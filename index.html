Session Day
FALL SEASON · SESSION 01 OF 08
Welcome to Up & At 'Em
90–100 minutes · In-person · CanU Rise Cohort
✓
Block 1 — Arrival & Welcome
0–15 min
⌄

As you arrive, find your seat. On your table is your Dream Board kit — don't start yet. We'll build it together.

Take a breath. Look around the room. These are your people for the next year.

"This room is yours. What gets said here, stays here. Nobody needs to perform or pretend."
2
Block 2 — Introductions
15–30 min
⌄

Share three things with the group:

1. Your name and what you actually like to be called.

2. One thing you're genuinely good at — even if it feels small.

3. One word that honestly describes how you feel walking in today.

No wrong answers. Your mentors go first — they're doing this too.
3
Block 3 — Cohort Agreements
30–40 min
⌄

Together you'll build the culture of this room.

"What would need to be true about this room for you to feel safe enough to be completely honest?"

Your answers become the agreements every person signs. That poster goes on the wall at every session all year.

4
Block 4 — The CanU Dream Board
40–80 min · The heart of Session 1
⌄

Build your Dream Board together, one section at a time. Music plays. Take your time. There are no right answers — only honest ones.

☁️ My Dream — Who do I want to become? Not a job. A person.

⭐ My Spark — What is undeniably, specifically mine? Why does it have to be me?

👤 Who Am I Right Now? Not who I'm supposed to be — who am I actually?

❓ Why Does This Matter? What's at stake if I don't become this person?

🌍 What Impact Will I Make? What does the world look like because I was in it?

When everyone is done, share one thing from your board — whatever you're ready to share.
5
Block 5 — CanUniverse Onboarding & Close
80–100 min
⌄

You're already here — which means you've done step one. Welcome to the CanUniverse.

1. Complete your profile and upload a photo of your Dream Board.

2. Connect with your mentor in the portal.

3. Find your first between-session journal prompt.

"What do I most want for my life right now — not what others want for me, but what do I actually want?"
